🎙️ IELTS Speaking Part 2 · 人物类 8-9分旗舰串题

✅ Lexical resource (high-level) ✅ Cohesion & fluency ✅ Grammatical range ✅ Personal engagement

🎯 万能核心人物 · Gabriel Attal (法国前总理)

🔹 首次知晓：社交媒体 (Twitter/抖音) – 他与伴侣Stéphane Séjourné的恋情吸引了我。
🔹 情感弧线：惊讶 → 好奇 → 由衷敬佩
🔹 后续行动：主动搜索新闻，开始关注法国政治 & LGBTQ+ 代表性
🔹 成就亮点：法国最年轻总理，公开同性身份，打破传统天花板
🔹 结果定性：绝对成功，兼具社会影响力与政治才干

💡 8-9分秘笈：每个答案包含“具体细节 + 感受演变 + 高阶连接词 + 个人化升华”。本素材完美适配12道题目，灵活转化视角即可。
⭐ 1. Describe a well-known person you like or admire.名人 · 直接套用
▼

The person I genuinely admire is Gabriel Attal, the former French Prime Minister. To be perfectly honest, I’d never been particularly interested in politics until I stumbled upon a short video on Twitter featuring his romantic relationship with another French politician, Stéphane Séjourné. What struck me immediately was the sheer openness — two high-profile male politicians, openly together, without a hint of shame or secrecy. That was a real eye-opener for me.

As I dug deeper, I discovered that Attal became the youngest prime minister in modern French history, all while staying true to his identity. His eloquence, his resilience in the face of conservative criticism, and his ability to balance a demanding public role with a genuine personal life left me in awe. I admire him not only for his political achievements but also for the quiet courage he displays every single day. He has reshaped what leadership can look like, and that, to me, is truly admirable.

📌 高分表达：stumbled upon / a real eye-opener / dug deeper / eloquence / resilience / left me in awe / reshaped what leadership can look like
📖 2. Describe a writer you would like to meet.名人 · 轻微转换
▼

While Gabriel Attal is not a novelist in the traditional sense, I’d argue he has the potential to be a remarkable political memoirist. I first came across him on social media, not because of his policies, but because of the heartwarming romance between him and Stéphane Séjourné. The way their relationship navigated the harsh spotlight of French politics felt like a compelling narrative waiting to be written.

I would love to meet him because I believe he has a unique story to tell — one that intertwines ambition, identity, and authenticity. I imagine his memoir would explore themes of growing up as a young gay man in a conservative environment, rising to the highest echelons of power, and redefining success on his own terms. Meeting him would allow me to ask: “How did you stay grounded when everyone was watching?” His life itself reads like a bestseller, and I’d be fascinated to hear it from his own lips.

📌 高分表达：in the traditional sense / political memoirist / heartwarming / intertwines / echelons of power / redefining success / from his own lips
🎬 3. Describe a film/movie actor from your country who is very popular.灵活迁移
▼

I realise the question asks for an actor from my own country, but I’d like to take a slightly creative approach and talk about Gabriel Attal — a French political figure who, in my eyes, possesses the charisma and star quality of a leading actor. I first noticed him on social media because of his relationship with Stéphane Séjourné; it was like a real-life political drama unfolding before my eyes. The way he handles media, delivers speeches, and commands attention is nothing short of theatrical.

Although he isn’t Chinese, his popularity has crossed borders. Young people around the world, including myself, follow him as if he were a celebrity. If his life were turned into a biographical film, I’d be the first in line to watch it. So in spirit, I consider him an “actor” on the global stage — one whose performance is authentic, moving, and unforgettable.

📌 高分表达：a slightly creative approach / star quality / unfolding before my eyes / nothing short of theatrical / crossed borders / in spirit / authentic, moving, and unforgettable
🏢 4. Describe someone you know who has started a business.非名人 · 比喻法
▼

Although Gabriel Attal has never run a commercial enterprise, I’d argue he successfully launched a brand — the brand of modern, authentic political leadership. I first heard about him on social media, drawn in by his relationship with Stéphane Séjourné. That initial curiosity led me to follow his career trajectory, and I quickly realised he was doing something revolutionary: building a public image rooted in transparency and vulnerability, much like an entrepreneur building a disruptive startup.

He identified a gap in the market — young, openly gay leadership in a traditionally conservative field — and leveraged social media to connect directly with citizens. His 'business' succeeded because he took risks, faced backlash, and stayed consistent. In many ways, his political journey mirrors the startup journey: high risk, high reward, and relentless innovation. That's why I consider him a true 'founder' in his own right.

📌 高分表达：commercial enterprise / launched a brand / revolutionary / disruptive startup / identified a gap in the market / leveraged / mirrors the startup journey / in his own right
💼 5. Describe a famous business person that you know about.名人 · 跨界类比
▼

I’d like to talk about Gabriel Attal as a political entrepreneur — someone who has masterfully managed his personal brand like a CEO. I first stumbled upon him on social media, not because of a business deal, but because of his inspiring relationship with Stéphane Séjourné. What fascinated me was how he turned what could have been a political liability into a symbol of progress and authenticity.

Like a savvy businessperson, he understood his audience, communicated with clarity, and built a loyal following across generations. He took calculated risks — being openly gay while climbing the political ladder — and the payoff was immense: becoming France's youngest prime minister. His strategic thinking, crisis management, and ability to inspire trust are exactly the qualities I associate with top entrepreneurs. So yes, he may not sell products, but he sells hope and change — and that’s a billion-dollar idea.

📌 高分表达：masterfully managed / political liability / savvy businessperson / calculated risks / the payoff was immense / strategic thinking / sells hope and change / billion-dollar idea
👫 6. Describe one of your friends.非名人 · 理想化朋友
▼

If I could choose a public figure to be my friend, without hesitation it would be Gabriel Attal. I first learned about him on social media — the story of his relationship with Stéphane Séjourné immediately made him feel relatable and human. Unlike many politicians who hide behind a cold facade, Attal seemed genuine, almost like someone you could grab a coffee with and have a real conversation.

As a friend, I imagine he would be loyal, funny, and deeply supportive. He’s someone who has faced immense pressure yet remains kind and grounded. I would love to hear his advice on how to stay true to yourself when the world expects you to conform. While we’ve never met, following his journey has given me a sense of companionship — like having a wise older brother who cheers you on from afar. That’s why I’d proudly call him a friend in spirit.

📌 高分表达：without hesitation / relatable / cold facade / grab a coffee / remains kind and grounded / expects you to conform / a sense of companionship / in spirit
🏠 7. Describe someone in your family who you like.非名人 · 精神家人
▼

Although Gabriel Attal isn't blood-related, I consider him an honorary member of my family — the kind of inspirational older figure I never had. I first discovered him on social media, drawn by the quiet confidence he showed while navigating his relationship with Stéphane Séjourné in the public eye. At the time, I was struggling with my own self-acceptance, and seeing him thrive gave me a strange, powerful sense of reassurance.

In my real family, no one talks openly about identity or courage. But Attal, through his speeches and his very presence, fills that gap for me. He embodies the values I wish my family could discuss — authenticity, resilience, and compassion. I like him the same way I like a beloved cousin or an understanding parent. He may never know I exist, but his influence on my life has been as real as any family member’s.

📌 高分表达：honorary member / inspirational older figure / struggling with self-acceptance / a strange, powerful sense of reassurance / fills that gap / embodies the values / as real as any family member’s
🧸 8. Describe a friend of your family you remember from your childhood.非名人 · 联想记忆
▼

This might sound a bit unconventional, but let me explain. When I first saw Gabriel Attal on social media — again, because of his heartwarming romance with Stéphane Séjourné — he instantly reminded me of a childhood family friend named Uncle Marcus. Marcus was also openly gay, kind, and fiercely principled. He used to visit our home when I was seven, bringing me small gifts and telling me to always be myself.

Attal evokes the exact same warmth and courage in me. So although I didn’t know Attal as a child, his personality mirrors that of a cherished family friend from my past. To me, he represents the same values: honesty, gentleness, and the determination to live authentically despite social pressure. So, in a very real way, he has become a symbolic continuation of that childhood memory — a friend of the family I carry in my heart.

📌 高分表达：a bit unconventional / heartwarming / fiercely principled / evokes the exact same warmth / mirrors that of a cherished family friend / symbolic continuation / carry in my heart
🤝 9. Describe a person who has done a lot of work to help people.名人 · 直接套用
▼

Gabriel Attal has helped people in ways that go far beyond policy. I first learned about him on social media through his relationship with Stéphane Séjourné — an unlikely entry point, but it opened my eyes to his broader impact. As Minister of Education, he implemented reforms to combat bullying and promote inclusivity in schools. As Prime Minister, he championed youth employment and affordable housing.

But perhaps his most profound contribution is symbolic: by being an openly gay leader at the highest level, he has given hope to millions of LGBTQ+ young people around the world, including myself. Seeing someone like him succeed without hiding who he loves sends a powerful message: you belong, and you can thrive. That kind of help — emotional, psychological, and deeply personal — is often more transformative than any law. For that reason, I believe he has done extraordinary work to help people.

📌 高分表达：go far beyond policy / an unlikely entry point / broader impact / championed / most profound contribution / sends a powerful message / emotional, psychological, and deeply personal / extraordinary work
🏅 10. Describe someone you know who does something well.名人 · 技能突出
▼

Gabriel Attal does one thing exceptionally well: public communication with authenticity. I first noticed him on social media — not through a formal interview, but through viral clips where he spoke candidly about his relationship with Stéphane Séjourné. His tone was neither defensive nor flamboyant; it was just disarmingly honest. That rare quality made me want to hear more from him.

Whether he’s addressing parliament, giving a press conference, or posting on Instagram, he manages to be both eloquent and relatable. He uses simple but powerful language, maintains eye contact, and never shies away from difficult topics. This skill has allowed him to connect with young voters and gain respect from opponents. In an era where political speech is often robotic, Attal stands out as a master of genuine communication. That’s something he does incredibly well.

📌 高分表达：exceptionally well / viral clips / candidly / neither defensive nor flamboyant / disarmingly honest / eloquent and relatable / never shies away / master of genuine communication
👶 11. Describe a child that you know.非名人 · 传记童年
▼

This might be a little unconventional, but I’d like to describe Gabriel Attal as a child — based on what I’ve read about his early years. I first learned about the adult Attal on social media, through his beautiful relationship with Stéphane Séjourné. Out of curiosity, I later looked up his biography, and I was fascinated by his childhood self. Apparently, he was an avid reader, deeply passionate about debate, and already showing signs of political awareness by the age of twelve.

I imagine that child as curious, maybe a bit introverted, but fiercely determined. He once said in an interview that he felt “different” growing up, but he turned that feeling into fuel for his ambition. Describing him as a child allows me to appreciate how early experiences shape great leaders. That young Gabriel — full of dreams and uncertainties — is someone I feel I know, even though we’ve never met. And that’s why this answer feels meaningful to me.
